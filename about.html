About

I (she/her) am a fifth-year Ph.D. candidate in atmospheric chemistry working with Daniel Jacob at Harvard University. I work to improve constraints on the magnitude and distribution of methane emission sources using high-resolution, analytic inversions of observations from the TROPOMI instrument aboard the Sentinel-5 Precursor satellite. I develop and apply methods to conduct these inversions at reduced computational cost while preserving information content.

In my free time, I serve as President of the Harvard GSAS Environmental Action Team (GrEAT), as a graduate student representative to the Harvard Earth and Planetary Sciences Diversity, Inclusion, and Belonging (DIB) Council, and as the co-lead of the Atmospheric Chemistry Modeling Group's DIB subgroup. I also enjoy running, hiking and camping, reading, and making bad jokes about methane (it is, after all, the chhhhillest molecule). I grew up in Minnesota and am happiest around small, medium, and large bodies of water.
