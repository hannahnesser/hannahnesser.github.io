About

I am a third-year Ph.D. candidate in atmospheric chemistry working with Daniel Jacob at Harvard University. I work to improve constraints on the magnitude and distribution of methane emission sources using high-resolution, analytic inversions of observations from the TROPOMI instrument aboard the Sentinel-5 Precursor satellite. I develop and apply methods to conduct these inversions at reduced computational cost while preserving information content.

Outside of research, I serve as President of the Harvard GSAS Environmental Action Team (GrEAT) and volunteer with the Harvard Environmental Law and Policy Center. When not at my desk, I enjoy running, hiking and camping, reading, and making bad jokes about methane (it is, after all, the chhhhillest molecule). I grew up in Minnesota, so I am happiest around small, medium, and large bodies of water.
